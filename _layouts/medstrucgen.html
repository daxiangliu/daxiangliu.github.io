Overview

From unconstrained prose to clinically verifiable findings.

Free-text generation can sound fluent while introducing ambiguity, hallucinated findings, or weak links to localized visual evidence. MedStrucGen-ML predicts discrete clinical findings and maps them to standardized templates, making every conclusion easier to trace and verify.

Why structured reporting?

Clinical statements should be explicit, not implied.

The framework turns anatomy-grounded predictions into a consistent report, reducing contradictions and making positive and negative findings directly inspectable.

Method

Global context, local anatomy, structured output.

Stage 1

Multi-grained extraction

A ResNet or ViT backbone captures global context while DGLR isolates anatomy-specific local evidence from detected regions.

Stage 2

Semantic refinement

Self-attention fuses image, local, and label tokens; gated CSCA then aligns each label with its mapped anatomical structure.

Stage 3

Template population

Predicted findings are resolved across mandibular states and populated into a standardized, clinically readable report.

Anatomical constraints remain effective across architectures.

The proposed framework achieves the best mAP under every evaluated backbone. The largest improvement appears with vision transformers, where explicit local evidence and class-specific alignment compensate for weaker anatomical inductive bias.

  • DGLR improves localization of subtle pathological regions.
  • Confidence-gated CSCA prevents noisy detections from dominating predictions.
  • Structured outputs avoid contradictions visible in free-text baselines.

Clinical Dataset

Anatomy-aware labels for two functional states.

Representative 2D TMJ MRI slices are annotated by clinical experts. The label space captures joint cavity, condyle, articular disc, and articular tubercle findings across closed- and open-mouth states.
